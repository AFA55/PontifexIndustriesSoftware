Pontifex — “P” Logo Concepts

Three directions for a modern P that subtly carries the bridge idea. Each shown as an iOS app icon (squircle on the indigo gradient), on white, and at favicon sizes — so we judge legibility where it actually lives. Front-runner per the council: Concept A.

FRONT-RUNNER

A · Arch-Counter P

A bold, confident P (Pandora-level presence). The clever part: the counter — the negative space inside the bowl — is shaped as a bridge arch (vertical piers, curved span, flat deck). The bridge isn’t drawn on top of the P; it is the hole in the P.

Reads as: solid, established, "real company." Bridge cue: the arch you see through it.

MODERN GRADIENT · LIGHT TILE

B · Arch-Counter P (Gradient)

The same winning letterform as A, in its modern treatment: an indigo→violet gradient mark on a white tile. This is the "lighter, software-forward" look — useful to compare directly against A’s bold flat dark icon. Pick the treatment you prefer; the underlying mark is identical.

Reads as: premium, current, app-store-modern. Bridge cue: same arch counter.

CLAUDE-STYLE MINIMAL

C · Monoline P

The restrained option: a single, even-weight stroke with round caps; the bowl reads as a gentle arch. Airy, modern, and the easiest to animate (the stroke can "draw" itself on launch — the bridge building itself) and to embroider/etch on crew gear.

Reads as: light, premium, software-first. Bridge cue: the arch of the bowl.
